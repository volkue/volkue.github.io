J. Robert Oppenheimer

father of the atomic bomb
From right: J. Robert Oppenheimer, Professor. H. D. Smythe, General Nichols in 1946 looking at a photograph of the atomic blast at Hiroshima. The atomic bomb was developed in the Manhattan Project.
Here's a time line of Oppenheimer's life:
  • 1904- born New York City on April 22
  • 1911-entered the Ethical Culture Society School
  • 1922-entered Harvard College
  • 1924-he had been accepted into Christ's College, Cambridge
  • 1926-left Cambridge for the University of Göttingen to study under Max Born
  • 1927-obtained his Doctor of Philosophy degree
  • 1942-the US Army established the Manhattan Project to handle its part in the atom bomb project and began the process of transferring responsibility from the Office of Scientific Research and Development to the military.[96] In September, Groves was appointed director of what became known as the Manhattan Project.[97] He selected Oppenheimer to head the project's secret weapons laboratory.
  • 1943-development efforts were directed to a plutonium gun-type fission weapon called "Thin Man"
  • 1944-Oppenheimer abandoned the gun design in favor of an implosion-type weapon
  • 1945-an Interim Committee was created to advise and report on wartime and postwar policies regarding the use of nuclear energy. The Interim Committee in turn established a scientific panel consisting of Arthur Compton, Fermi, Lawrence and Oppenheimer to advise it on scientific issues
  • 1946-for his services as director of Los Alamos, Oppenheimer was awarded the Medal for Merit from President Harry S Truman
  • 1967-Oppenheimer was a chain smoker who was diagnosed with throat cancer.He fell into a coma on February 15, 1967, and died at his home in Princeton, New Jersey, on February 18
